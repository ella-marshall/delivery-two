Two Provinces, One Visit

Salta and Jujuy border each other, so be sure to combine them during your visit. The easiest way is to fly into one airport and out of the other. This will ensure that you spend the least amount of time in the car.

Highlights

Salta

Salta features landscapes similar to the American southwest. Canyons, mountains, and rock formations in deep red tones make for an incredible car ride.
One famous canyon called the Garganta del Diablo (pictured above) features pinks, yellows, oranges, and reds in its stunning landscape.
During your drives in Salta, miradores along the highway wait to greet you with spectacular views.

Jujuy

Jujuy is a classic desert, filled with cacti and rocks. The Andes are on full display with their remarkably high peaks dotting Jujuy's landscape.
Don't miss the Cerro de Los Catorce Colores! This lookout features a unique triangular mountainside with vivid colors at a dizzying altitude of 4,350 meters.
The Salinas Grandes are also in Jujuy (and Salta). These salt flats stretch for many kilometers and provide a unique environment to enjoy.
